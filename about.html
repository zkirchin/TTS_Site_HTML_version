Our Story

The story of Tech Talent South starts with two southerners meeting halfway across the country in the windy city of Chicago. Hailing from Charlotte, NC, Betsy Hauser Idilbi had just sold off her Charlotte-based physical product development company and was looking for her next adventure. An Atlanta, GA native, Richard Simms was riding the entrepreneurial roller coster working with a Chicago-based early stage tech startup. Coincidently, both Betsy and Richard had hit a point where they realized they needed to learn to "code." For Betsy, with her background in helping entrepreneurs build physical products and launch companies, the exciting world of tech and the low cost of starting web-based businesses was too hard to resist. As Richard worked with developers to get a SaaS product built from scratch, he began to recognize the necessity of being able to talk the talk, prototype ideas, and understand how products were built.

So, the two enrolled in some programming classes in Chicago, determined to master this empowering skill of coding. As the only two southerners in the class and with a shared passion for startups and all things tech, Betsy and Richard quickly became friends. Seeing the tremendous positive impact "coding bootcamps" were having, developing more technical talent and empowering entrepreneurs in places like California and New York, the two couldn't help but ask "Why the heck don't these opportunities exist in the South?" As they say, the rest is history. Betsy and Richard set out on a mission to bring tech education back home and to fuel growth and innovation in the South.

With TTS's core "Code Immersion Programs," students are taken through an intensive but beginner-friendly 8-weeks learning a number of programming languages such as Ruby on Rails, HTML/CSS, Javascript, and AJAX. The goal? To go from beginner to being able to code and deploy dynamic web applications and to have a blast in the process. With networking opportunities, guest speakers, labs and company tours students are given ample opportunities to build valuable connections in the local tech community.

Today, the TTS team and impact has grown by leaps and bounds, unified in the mission to develop more tech talent in the South and empower folks on a mission to do something BIG. After finding success in its initial markets, TTS has continued to set its sights on city after city in the South where it feels it can have a positive impact on the tech community and do its part to fuel innovation. With hundreds of alumni throughout the South, TTS has graduates working as developers, starting companies, pouring their hearts into local startups, publishing apps to the Apple app store, and more. By developing much-needed tech talent from the ground up, TTS believes that over time the value and impact of its programs will only continue to grow.
